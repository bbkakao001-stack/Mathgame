7.1

Powers of 10

Multiply & divide by 10ⁿ

📖 Key Terms

Powers & Indices

Definition

A power or index tells you how many copies of a number to multiply together.

10⁴ = 10 × 10 × 10 × 10 = 10 000
Tip

10¹ = 10 and 10⁰ = 1.

Did you know?

10¹⁰⁰ is called a googol. The search engine Google was named after this huge number! 🔍

✏️ Worked Example 1

Multiply & Divide by 10ⁿ

a) 10⁰b) 10⁴c) 1000 as power
d) 36 × 10⁴e) 0.036 × 10⁴f) 0.2 ÷ 10⁴
✅ Answers

Worked Example 1 — Solution

a) 10⁰ = 1 b) 10⁴ = 10 000 c) 1000 = 10³
d) 36 × 10⁴ = 360 000
e) 0.036 × 10⁴ = 360 f) 0.2 ÷ 10⁴ = 0.00002
Method

Multiplying moves digits left; dividing moves digits right. Fill gaps with zeros.

7.2

Rounding

To decimal places

📖 Key Terms

Decimal Places

Decimal place (d.p.)

A space after the decimal point where you write a digit. 3.142 has 3 d.p.

Accurate

Close to the true value. Rounding usually makes a number less accurate.

Tip

1 d.p. = nearest tenth · 2 d.p. = nearest hundredth · 3 d.p. = nearest thousandth.

Division Words

Divisor = 10 (number you divide by)
Quotient = 5 (the answer)
Remainder = 6

⭐ Chapter 7 Reflection

Key Ideas 🔑

  • Multiplying by 10ⁿ = multiplying by ten n times.
  • To estimate, round each number so you can calculate mentally.
  • If an answer is negative, swap order, calculate, add a minus sign.
  • For long multiplication with a decimal: ignore the point, multiply, then place the point.
  • For division to n d.p., work out one extra digit to decide rounding.
✅ Skills Checklist

You Should Be Able To…

  • Multiply & divide integers and decimals by powers of 10.
  • Estimate adding & subtracting decimals.
  • Estimate multiplying or dividing a decimal by an integer.
  • Add & subtract decimals (including negative answers).
  • Multiply a decimal by an integer.
  • Divide a decimal by a 1- or 2-digit integer.
  • Divide and write the answer to a specified number of d.p.
